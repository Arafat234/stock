Frequently Asked Questions?
Why we focus on the FTSE 100 stock market ?

We focus on the FTSE 100 market because it contains some of the biggest companies worldwide that generate enormous profits anually. Overtime we will look to expand into different markets to provide further analysis.

What metrics do you offer and what do they mean ?
  1. Symbol

    2. The symbol represents the short term abbreviation by which a company is recognised.

     
  2. Ticker

    3. Displays the company's name.

     
  3. Current Price

    4. Displays a stocks current price for a particular company on the given trading day.

     
  4. Latest Open Price

    5. The starting price of a stock when the FTSE 100 market opens.

     
  5. Latest High Price

    6. The highest price of a stock sold on a particular trading day.

     
  6. Latest Low Price

    7. The Lowest price of a stock sold on a particular trading day.

     
  7. Latest Close Price

    8. The last price the stock was sold for on a particular trading day.

     
  8. Volume

    9. A stock's volume refers to the number of shares that are sold, or traded, over a certain period of time (usually daily).

     
  9. Currency

    10. The currency in which the stock is traded with for the FTSE 100 market.

     
  10. Change Percentage

    11. The change percentage is the percentage generated from how much the current price of a stock has changed since its last trade.

     
  11. Volume Average

    12. The total amount a specific stock has traded over a particular time period.

     
  12. Total number of shares

    13. The total amount of shares in which a company of the FTSE 100 market is currently made up of.

     
  13. Last Trade Time

    14. The last time a stock for a particular company has been traded at.

     
  14. Timezone

    15. The time zone used to support the Last Trade Time.

     
  15. 52 Week High

    16. The highest price a stock was sold at over the last 52 weeks.

     
  16. 52 Week Low

    17. The lowest price a stock was sold at over the last 52 weeks.

     
  17. Close Probabilty for Specified date

    18. A special metric we had designed to help provide a prediction on whether stock closing price will either increase,decrease or stay the same.

     
How to start trading for beginners?
  1. Open up a brokerage account
    2.  

    A brokerage account is an account used to hold money for investmenets in future stocks.

     
  2. Set a specific budget
    3.  

    When trading you never want to exceed the amount of money you invest into stocks, its important to keep a consistent budget that you can maintain.

     
  3. Learn to use market orders and limit orders
    4.  

    With a brokerage account and budget in place, you can a online broker's website or trading platform to place your stock trades.When dealing with a broker you will have two types of ways to choose how your stock will be dealt with.

    • Market order: Buy or sell stocks immediately at the best price.
      •  
    • Limit order: Buy or sell stocks at a specific price different to the price you specified originally.

     
  4. Use a practice account
    5.  

    Doing this is will help you understand how the market works and to see if your trading strategy is useful.In this way you will only learn and get better at trading.
